Citation and Data Use Policy

Corrosion Atlas is an independently curated academic platform for corrosion exposure-site data. The platform organizes site locations, environmental classifications, metallic materials, exposure periods, corrosion-rate records, and source evidence into a searchable geospatial interface.

Platform status

Corrosion Atlas is currently an active research and data-curation project. A versioned dataset release and formal DOI-linked citation may be provided in the future.

How to cite this platform

If this platform, its curated site records, harmonized metadata, or geospatial organization are used in academic work, users should cite Corrosion Atlas as the curated database and mapping platform.

Suggested citation:

Corrosion Atlas Project. Corrosion Atlas: A curated geospatial platform for corrosion exposure sites, materials, exposure periods, corrosion-rate records, and source evidence. Available at: https://corrosionatlas.org. Accessed: [date].

Original source citation

Corrosion Atlas does not replace the original literature, reports, standards, datasets, or exposure-programme documents from which primary corrosion data were reported. Users should cite both the original source document and Corrosion Atlas when using curated records from this platform.

Acceptable use

Users are welcome to use the platform for academic research, teaching, literature review, corrosion-data comparison, and non-commercial scholarly analysis, provided that appropriate attribution is given to both the original sources and this platform.

Unacceptable use

Automated bulk extraction, redistribution of the compiled database without attribution, removal of source-traceability information, misrepresentation of curated records as independently produced data, or commercial reuse without permission is not permitted.

Data accuracy and limitations

The database is curated from published and documented sources. Site names, country labels, coordinates, exposure periods, material descriptions, and environmental classifications may involve harmonization or interpretation. Users should verify critical values against the original source documents before relying on them for design, safety assessment, or regulatory decisions.

Corrections and contributions

Corrections, source suggestions, and collaboration inquiries are welcome. A formal contribution procedure will be added as the platform develops.
